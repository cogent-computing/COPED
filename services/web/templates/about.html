About CoPED - Catalogue of Projects on Energy Data

Problem Statement

Currently it is not possible for energy data users and related stakeholders to know and make sense of the multitude of data and digitalisation related activity in the energy sector. This inevitably leads missed opportunities, effort duplication and counter-productive time expenditure to navigate a complex projects and data landscape.

Efforts to gather and organise information about energy data and specific activities on digitalization have lacked coordination and have been incomplete. Information on actors in the landscape, project funders, timelines, data generated is often difficult to locate. Furthermore, stakeholders have no effective way to maintain and update information they might have gathered or create bespoke views of the landscape that are relevant to their needs. The value of any such “landscape information” rapidly diminishes if not accurate or user friendly.

A tool that captures investment, outputs, outcomes and key players and funders for energy projects that address energy digitization is instrumental to the decarbonization efforts.

Project Scope

COPE, or the Catalogue of Projects on Energy project aims to unify various information stores and existing portals for energy projects under a single extendible umbrella that has two key roles:

1. Update, curate and correct information pertaining to the existing energy project landscape through manual and automated processes.

2. Offer a wide range of visual aids, query tools and metrics that enable users to synthesize information across the energy projects landscape at a given time.

COPE needs to provide diverse insights to various user groups while being able to continuously expand its data capture sources and the analytics and visualisations it can perform. As a platform, COPE needs to be able to evolve. As COPE reaches maturity and wide adoption, the product needs to allow individuals and institutions to build extensions and add features to the product rather than just upload or curate dat.

References

The data management strategy and governance needs to adhere to the UK Government guidelines on Smart Data – Link. This ensures that the project adheres to the same guides as other catalogues in the area.

The metadata standards used need to provide interface or connectivity extension that also adhere to the UK Governments guidelines on metadata which proposes the use of Dublin Core and DCAT like Resource Description Framework (RDF) standards. – Link. Adhering to existing metadata standards, or at least providing API’s for them allows the platform to be connected into a wider catalogue of data-sets.

A starting point for the metadata backbone of the catalogue could be the use of existing research such as the review done in the SEMANCO project. A simple example can be seen here. Using a standard that is based on years of research can speed up development and ensure integration in later phases.

The strategic intelligence graph from the World Economic Forum (WEF) is a good starting point for the visualisation. It shows how powerful the right visualisation can be in disseminating information, and the links between resources.
